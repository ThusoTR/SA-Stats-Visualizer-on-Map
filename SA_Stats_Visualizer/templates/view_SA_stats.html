Population Statistics


Figures 1 to 8 demostrate an overview of population statistics of South Africa. Plots that demonstrate the population growth, life expectancy, etc. are provided.


Figure 1: Population growth trends of South Africa

The estimated population growth trends of South Africa is demontstrated in figure 1. The estimated population of South Africa grew from 46 million in 2002, to 59 million in 2020.


Figure 2: Population by age group and sex

Figure 2 demonstates the South African 2020 mid-year estimated population by age group and sex. More than 60% of South Africa's population is made up of children aged 0 - 14 years and adults aged 15 - 34 years. At older ages, there is a surplus of females to males.


Figure 3: Population estimate by population group and sex

The Black African population group constitutes approximately 80.8 % of the total population of South Africa. The White, Indian/Asian, and Coloured population groups constitutes 7.8 %, 2.6%, and 8.8 % respectively.


Figure 4: Population estimate by province

Gauteng and Kwazulu-Natal are two most populuous provinces in South Africa, each with a population above 15 million and 11 million respectively. 26 % of the South African population lives in Gauteng province.


Figure 5: Births and deaths over time

South Africa receives over 1 million births and experiences over 500 thousand deaths annually. Between 2002 and 2009, over 200 thousand of the total deaths were AIDS related. This value has decreased to aprroximately 80 thousand in 2019.


Figure 6: HIV related deaths trends (2002 to mid-year 2020)

The percentage of the AIDS related deaths has decreased from a peak of 38 % in 2006 to just over 15% in 2019. This shows that the ARV drugs program has helped reduce the number of AIDS related deaths and pro-long the lives of people living with HIV.


Figure 7: Assummed life expectancy trends (2002 to mid-year 2020)

Between 2002 and mid-year 2020, the assummed life expectancy (if born without HIV/AIDS) of both males and females has increased. The assumed life expectancy of females is 71.3 years, which 6.7 years more than the 64.6 years assummed life expancy of males in South Africa.


Figure 8: Net international migration trends

From 2016 to 2021, South Africa is expected to experience a net international migration of approximately 800 thousand. Since 1985, South Africa has consistently experienced positive international net migration in the Black African, Coloured, and Indian/Asian population groups. The White population group has consistently experienced a negative international net migration since 1985.


Economic Statistics


Figures 9 to 16 demostrate an overview of economic statistics of South Africa. Plots that demonstrate the the GDP growth, unemployment trends, etc. are provided.


Figure 9: GDP growth trends of South Africa

In 2019, South Africa had a year on year GDP growth of 0.2 %. Between 2005 and 2007, South Africa experienced a GDP growth above 5 %.


Figure 10: Working age population per province

In terms of working age population, Gauteng and Kwazulu-Natal are two most populuous provinces in South Africa, each with a working age population above 10 million and 7 Million respectively.


Figure 11: Working age population per population group

South Africa has a working-age population of apporoximately 38.8 million. The Black African population group contributes approximately 31.4 million people in the working age population. The Coloured, Indian/Asian, and White population groups contribute approximately 3.5 million, 2.9 million, and 1 million respectively to the working age population.


Figure 12: Unemployment trends of South Africa

The unemployment (narrow definition) rate of South Africa has increased from approximate 22 % in 2008 to approximately 30 % in March 2020.


Figure 13: Unemployment trends of South Africa

The unemployment (expanded definition) rate of South Africa has increased from approximate 27 % in 2008 to approximately 40 % in March 2020. This means that out of the approximately 38 million people in the working age population, only 16 million are employed, 10 million are unemployed, and approximately 12 million are not economically active.


Figure 14: Unemployment by age group

Figure 14 shows that youths are the most affected by high unemployment rates. The unemployment (expaned definition) of youths in the age group 15-24 and 25 - 34 years in March 2020 was approximately 70 % and 47 % respectively. These figures are well above the national average of 39.7 % and result in a youth unemployment rate of apporoximately 58 %.


Figure 15: Unemployment per population group

The Black African and Coloured population groups are the most affected by unemployment, each with an unemployment (expanded definition) rate of 44 % and 31 % respectively.


Figure 16: Unemployment per province

The province of the Western Cape has the lowest unemployment (expaned definition) rate, sittimg at 25 %. All other provinces except Gauteng (33 %) have an unemployment rate that is at least 40 %. The Eastern Cape has the highest unemployment rate, sitting at 48 %.
